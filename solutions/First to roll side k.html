A and B are playing the following game: a number k from 1-6 is chosen, and A and B will toss a die until the first person sees the side k, and that person gets $100. How much is A willing to pay to play first in this game? We will assume the dice is fair.
We can model this using a geometric distribution.
The probability of winning if you're going first is
$\begin{align} \sum_{i~odd}^{\infty}(5/6)^{i-1}(1/6) &= (1/6)(1 + (5/6)^2 + (5/6)^4 + ...)\\ &= (1/6)\sum_{i=0}^{\infty}((5/6)^2)^i \\ &= (1/6)\frac{1}{1-(5/6)^2}\\ &= (1/6)\frac{6^2}{6^2-5^2}\\ &= \frac{6}{36-25}\\ &= \frac{6}{11} \end{align}$ Thus the expected winnings of the person going first is $100 * (\frac{6}{11}) \approx 54.55$ dollars.
It follows that A would pay up to $4.55 to go first.
